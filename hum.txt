## **Humanity Safety — Community Emergency Response Network**

---

# **1. Introduction**

Humanity Safety is an open-source community emergency response system designed to provide immediate human support during dangerous situations. The platform connects people through a simple SOS signal that instantly alerts volunteers and communities nearby. Instead of depending only on formal emergency services, the system activates a network of citizens who can respond quickly within the **golden hour**, when timely assistance is most critical. The goal is to transform isolated emergencies into visible community events where people coordinate and provide support.

---

# **2. Problem**

Many emergencies escalate because help does not arrive quickly. Victims may not be able to call authorities immediately, nearby people may be unaware of the situation, and emergency services may take time to respond. In these moments, individuals often face danger alone despite being surrounded by people who could help if they knew.

---

# **3. Key Problems Identified**

* Victims may be unable to call for help during emergencies
* Nearby people are often unaware that help is needed
* Emergency services may require time to reach the location
* Many safety systems depend only on official responders
* Personal safety apps often store excessive personal data
* Lack of coordination among people willing to help

---

# **4. Proposed Solution**

Humanity Safety introduces a **community-based emergency network**. When an emergency occurs, the system broadcasts an alert to volunteers and connected communities. Responders can communicate, coordinate movement toward the location, and assist the victim directly. The platform focuses on speed, simplicity, and minimal data sharing, ensuring rapid community awareness during critical moments.

---

# **5. Core Principles**

The system operates based on several important principles:

* **Community participation** – anyone using the app can help
* **Immediate alert broadcasting** – emergencies become visible instantly
* **Golden hour response** – the system prioritizes rapid action within the first hour
* **Minimal data usage** – only essential information is shared during emergencies
* **Temporary sessions** – all sensitive data is removed after resolution

---

# **6. Information Shared During Emergency**

Only essential information is shared to allow responders to act effectively:

* Name entered during setup
* Accurate GPS location of the device
* Emergency session ID
* Time of alert
* Type of alert (self SOS or crowd report)

No phone numbers or personal history are stored. The focus is on **accurate location and rapid awareness**.

---

# **7. Emergency Trigger Types**

The system provides two ways to activate the emergency network.

### **SOS (Self Emergency)**

Used when a person is in danger and requests help directly.

### **Help Someone (Crowd Alert)**

Used when a bystander witnesses an emergency and reports it for the victim.

This ensures emergencies can be reported even if the victim cannot use the app.

---

# **8. Volunteer Response Structure**

To maintain organization, the response system is structured.

### **Guiders**

The first **three volunteers** who respond become **Guiders**.
They communicate with the victim, understand the situation, and coordinate actions.

Guiders can also **call the victim directly through the app using an internet voice call**.
This call works through **VoIP technology (WebRTC)**, allowing voice communication without using phone numbers. The server connects both devices and creates a secure voice channel so the guider can guide, calm, and understand the victim’s condition.

### **Responders**

All other volunteers can join the session as community responders. They can:

* Chat with other responders
* Observe updates
* Move toward the location if nearby
* Assist based on guider instructions

This structure prevents confusion while still allowing many people to participate.

---

# **9. Multi-Volunteer Monitoring**

Multiple volunteers can monitor the emergency simultaneously. The session provides shared information such as:

* Victim location
* Number of responders
* Movement status of volunteers
* Situation updates

Volunteers can mark themselves as **moving toward the location**, allowing guiders to track approaching assistance.

The communication system also includes:

* **Live session chat** for coordination between volunteers
* **Internet voice call** between guiders and the victim
* Quick updates from responders (for example: *reached location*, *moving*, *need medical help*)

This allows volunteers to coordinate effectively while keeping the session organized.

---

# **10. Community Broadcast Escalation**

If volunteer response is insufficient, the system automatically expands visibility.

Possible escalation channels include:

* Regional emergency feed inside the app
* Public emergency channels such as Telegram
* Registered community networks

These broadcasts increase the chance that someone nearby notices the emergency and responds quickly.

---

# **11. Golden Hour Response Model**

The system is designed around the critical first hour of an emergency.

**0–1 minute:** SOS alert broadcast
**1–3 minutes:** Guiders establish contact with victim
**3–10 minutes:** Volunteers coordinate and move toward location
**10–30 minutes:** Situation stabilized through guidance or physical assistance
**30–60 minutes:** Emergency resolved and session closed

The goal is rapid awareness and action within the **golden hour**.

---

# **12. Emergency Resolution**

An emergency session ends when:

* The victim presses **SAFE**
* Responders confirm **END** after the situation is resolved

Once the emergency is concluded, the system sends a **session summary** and removes all personal session data.

---

# **13. Privacy and Abuse Protection**

Humanity Safety prioritizes privacy and responsible usage.

* Personal data remains on the device until an alert is triggered
* Only essential emergency information is shared
* All session data is deleted after the emergency ends
* The system stores only anonymous statistical counts
* A device can trigger a **maximum of five alerts per day**
* Devices exceeding this limit are **restricted from sending alerts but can still receive alerts**

This ensures both **user safety and privacy protection**.

---

# **14. System Limitations**

The current version of Humanity Safety requires **internet connectivity** to send SOS alerts and communicate with responders. Offline emergency signaling may be implemented in future versions.

---

# **15. Open Source Vision**

Humanity Safety is designed as an **open-source platform developed by the community and maintained by contributors worldwide**. This approach ensures transparency, encourages innovation, and allows communities to improve the system collaboratively.

---

# **16. System Philosophy**

Humanity Safety is designed as a **community-powered emergency support network**, similar in spirit to volunteer organizations where citizens assist one another during crises. The system does not replace official emergency services but fills the critical gap before professional responders arrive.

---

# **17. Conclusion**

Humanity Safety transforms emergency response from an isolated experience into a coordinated community effort. By enabling rapid alerts, structured volunteer coordination, and widespread awareness, the platform increases the chances of timely assistance during critical moments. Built as an open-source project, it encourages collaboration and participation from communities everywhere. Its mission is simple: ensure that when someone faces danger, help can emerge quickly from the people around them. 🛡️
