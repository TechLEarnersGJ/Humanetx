The Challenge

The Problem We Solve

Many emergencies escalate because help doesn't arrive quickly enough. We identified three core failures in current emergency response.

🔇

Victim Isolation

During emergencies, victims are often surrounded by people who could help but are completely unaware of the situation happening nearby.

⏱️

The Critical Response Gap

Official emergency services are vital but can take crucial minutes to arrive. The Golden Hour is when immediate help matters most.

🔀

Lack of Coordination

Even when people are willing to help, there is no system to coordinate their actions effectively, safely, and without confusion.

Two Ways to Act

Two Types of Alerts

Emergencies can be reported by the victim or by a bystander, ensuring no one is left without help.

🚨
SOS

Self SOS

Used when you are in danger and need immediate help. One tap broadcasts your name, location, and alert to all nearby volunteers.

  • Direct emergency broadcast
  • GPS location shared instantly
  • WebRTC voice call with Guiders
  • Mark yourself SAFE to end session
📢
CROWD

Crowd Alert

Used when you witness someone else in danger. Report the incident to the community awareness feed so nearby volunteers can respond.

  • Bystander emergency report
  • Incident added to public feed
  • Community awareness broadcast
  • Responders confirm END to close
The Golden Hour

The Response Timeline

Every second counts. Our system is designed around the critical first 60 minutes of an emergency — the Golden Hour.

0 – 1 Minute

Broadcast Phase

An SOS alert is broadcast to all volunteers within range. Push notifications are sent immediately via the network.

1 – 3 Minutes

Contact Phase

The first three volunteers become Guiders. They establish a secure WebRTC voice call with the victim to provide immediate support.

3 – 10 Minutes

Coordination Phase

Guiders and Responders coordinate via live chat, sharing updates and tracking movement on a shared map in real-time.

10 – 30 Minutes

Stabilization Phase

The community stabilizes the situation through physical assistance or guidance until professional help arrives.

30 – 60 Minutes

Resolution Phase

The emergency is resolved when the victim presses SAFE or responders confirm END. All sensitive session data is permanently deleted.

Who Responds

Community Roles

A structured response prevents confusion while allowing many people to participate effectively.

🛡️

Guiders

The first three volunteers to respond become Guiders. They lead the response — communicating with the victim via secure VoIP, coordinating other responders, and providing guidance.

  • Direct WebRTC voice call to victim
  • Lead coordination in live chat
  • Assess situation and direct responders
🦸

Responders

All other volunteers join as Responders. They assist Guiders, provide on-the-ground support, and help secure the area — coordinating through the live session chat.

  • Join session and view updates
  • Move toward location if nearby
  • Assist under Guider instructions
Built for Safety

Core Features

Every feature is designed for speed, security, and simplicity during the moments that matter most.

📞

WebRTC Voice Calls

Guiders connect to victims via secure internet voice calls — no phone numbers exchanged, ever.

📍

GPS Tracking

Accurate device location is shared during emergencies so responders can navigate directly to the victim.

💬

Live Chat

Real-time session chat lets Guiders and Responders coordinate movement, share updates, and track progress.

📡

Escalation System

If volunteer response is insufficient, alerts automatically escalate to regional feeds and community channels.

🔒

Privacy First

Minimal data sharing during emergencies. All session data is permanently deleted upon resolution.

🌐

Open Source

Built by the community, for the community. Transparent, auditable, and continuously improving through open collaboration.

Your Safety, Your Data

Your Data, Protected

We share only what's needed and never store what's not. All session data is deleted permanently upon resolution.

✅ What We Share

  • Name entered during setup
  • GPS coordinates of device
  • Emergency session ID
  • Time of alert
  • Alert type (SOS or Crowd)

🚫 What We Never Store

  • Phone numbers
  • Personal history
  • Device identifiers
  • Post-session location data
  • Conversation recordings
